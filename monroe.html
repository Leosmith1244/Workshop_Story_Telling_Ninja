Monroe's Motivated Sequence

The PR Professional's Persuasion Framework

Our Speech Framework This Month

Exploring Monroe's proven 5-step persuasive structure

This month at PR Pros Toastmasters, we're focusing on Monroe's Motivated Sequence, the gold standard for persuasive communication. Developed in the 1930s by Alan H. Monroe, this five-step structure is particularly powerful for PR professionals who need to craft messages that inspire action.

Why are we focusing on this now? Because in public relations, we don't just share information—we persuade, we influence, and we drive action. Monroe's sequence gives us a hypnotic framework to do just that.

Perfect for PR Professionals

  • Craft compelling press releases and pitches
  • Influence stakeholder perceptions
  • Drive measurable campaign outcomes
  • Deliver persuasive speeches and presentations

The 5 Steps of Monroe's Sequence

How we're applying this framework in our speeches

1

Attention

Grab your audience immediately with a striking opening—a startling fact, provocative question, or vivid story.

PR Application:

"Did you know 80% of press releases never get read past the headline?"

2

Need

Identify a problem your audience cares about. Create urgency by showing why this matters now.

PR Application:

"Without media coverage, even the best products fail to reach their potential customers."

3

Satisfaction

Present your solution clearly. Show how it specifically addresses the need you've identified.

PR Application:

"Our media pitching framework increases coverage by 300% by focusing on journalist needs."

4

Visualization

Help your audience see the future—either the benefits of your solution or consequences of inaction.

PR Application:

"Imagine your product featured in every major industry publication—that's the power of strategic PR."

5

Action

Give clear, specific steps your audience can take immediately. Make it easy to say "yes."

PR Application:

"Start today—download our free pitching template and send your first story idea by noon."

Monroe's Sequence in Famous PR Campaigns

How top brands use this framework to persuade millions

Nike's "Just Do It" Campaign

One of the most successful campaigns in history followed Monroe's sequence perfectly:

Attention

Powerful visuals of athletes pushing limits with inspirational music

Need

Tapped into universal desire to overcome limitations

Satisfaction

Positioned Nike products as tools for achievement

Visualization

Showed everyday people achieving extraordinary feats

Action

"Just Do It" slogan urged immediate purchase

Dove's "Real Beauty" Campaign

Changed beauty industry standards using Monroe's framework:

Attention

Emotional stories of women's insecurities

Need

Highlighted unrealistic beauty standards

Satisfaction

Promoted self-esteem and inclusive products

Visualization

Showed women feeling confident and empowered

Action

Invited joining Self-Esteem Project
